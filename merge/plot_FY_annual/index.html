GSA Energy Saving Analysis

 

Introduction: Executive Orders and GSA's Energy Goals

Launched in December 2007, "Energy Independence and Security Act (EISA)" stated energy reduction goals for federal agencies.[source]

In the Executive Order 13423, several energy reduction goals were described as:

  • "reduction of energy intensity by (i) 3 percent annually through the end of fiscal year 2015, or (ii) 30 percent by the end of fiscal year 2015, relative to the baseline of the agency’s energy use in fiscal year 2003"
  • "beginning in FY 2008, reduce water consumption intensity, relative to the baseline of the agency’s water consumption in fiscal year 2007, through life-cycle cost-effective measures by 2 percent annually through the end of fiscal year 2015 or 16 percent by the end of fiscal year 2015"

In the 42 U.S.C. § 8253(a) the rule stated that:

Each agency shall apply energy conservation measures to, and shall improve the design for the construction of, the Federal buildings of the agency (including each industrial or laboratory facility) so that the energy consumption per gross square foot of the Federal buildings of the agency in fiscal years 2006 through 2015 is reduced, as compared with the energy consumption per gross square foot of the Federal buildings of the agency in fiscal year 2003, by the percentage specified in the following table

Fiscal Year Percentage Reduction
2006 2
2007 4
2008 9
2009 12
2010 15
2011 18
2012 21
2013 24
2014 27
2015 30
 

Stated goals and objectives

This data driven study will support the documentation and categorization of the key areas of operational and capital investments that generated the US General Services Administration energy intensity. The in-depth analysis could provide critical insights for strategic recommendations for ongoing investments in capital and operational energy upgrades in GSA's owned portfolio. The tasks include:

  1. Qualification and Quantification of historical energy savings in relation to energy upgrade investments in 3 Tiers of Analysis

    2. Database development

    Carnegie Mellon uses Sqlite to hold energy data, static information, weather data, as well as available energy upgrade ECM records. The data sets are unified around building IDs ("Building_Number"). This consolidated data set will be used in all three levels of analysis.

    Three tiers analysis

    The three tiers of analysis correspond to the depth of data available from the GSA.

    • Level 1 analysis

      Level 1 analysis addresses the full portfolio of GSA buildings for which there is monthly EUAS data from 2003 to 2015, between 1000 and 1500 buildings. Level 1 analysis include:

      • GSA portfolio overview and data quality check
      • Total energy usage trend
      • EUI distribution trend (A + I, covered facility, capital vs operational group)
    • Level 2 analysis

      Level 2 analysis addresses a subset of this portfolio, between 200 and 400 buildings for which there is additionally ECM investment records and robust energy data. Level 2 analysis include:

      • EUI comparison FY2003 vs FY2015 by energy investment type
      • Weather normalized saving by ECM actions
    • Level 3 analysis

      Level 3 analysis addresses a final set of buildings, between 50 and 81 buildings, for which there is 15min interval data (and potentially BAS data from SkyFoundry’s SkySpark). Level 3 analysis include

      • Data quality check
      • Compared Lean Energy Analysis of week day vs weekend and night energy consumption pattern

  2. Strategic recommendations for ongoing operational and capital investments

    3. CMU's Center for Building Performance and Diagnostics will use these tiered data sources to generate data driven recommendations for future operational and capital investments to ensure ongoing success in meeting commitments under the Energy Independence and Security Act.
