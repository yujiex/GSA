Glossary of GSA Energy Saving Analysis

Boxplot (box-and-whisker)

In descriptive statistics, a box plot or boxplot is a convenient way of graphically depicting groups of numerical data through their quartiles. Box plots may also have lines extending vertically from the boxes (whiskers) indicating variability outside the upper and lower quartiles [source]. It is a histogram-like method of displaying data, invented by J. Tukey [source]. In the plot below, M is the median, Q1 is the 25th percentile (first quartile), and Q2 is the 75th percentile (third quartile).

Building Energy Intensity Reduction Goal/Requirement [source]

Subject to certain exclusions, each Federal agency must reduce the energy consumption per gross square foot of its Federal buildings relative to a fiscal year (FY) 2003 baseline by 27% by 2014 and by 30% by FY 2015

CV(RMSE), or coefficient of variation of the RMSD

Root-mean-square error normalized by mean of the data.
[source: ASHRAE Guideline 14-2014]
The root-mean-square error (RMSE) represents the sample standard deviation of the differences between predicted values and observed values. It is a good measure of accuracy, but only to compare forecasting errors of different models for a particular variable and not between variables, as it is scale-dependent. Normalizing the RMSD facilitates the comparison between datasets or models with different scales. Though there is no consistent means of normalization in the literature, common choices are the mean or the range. When normalizing with mean, the turn is called CV(RMSE). [source]

EUI (Energy Use Intensity) [source]

Energy consumption per area for a building. The unit used in this study is kBtu/sq.ft

Interval data [source]

Interval energy data is a fine-grained record of energy consumption, with readings made at regular intervals. The interval data (gas and electricity) used in the study is with 15-min interval.
