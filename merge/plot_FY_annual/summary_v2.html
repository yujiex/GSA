Summary of level 1

    GSA has 1000 - 1500 buildings in their portfolio with multiple year energy monitoring at some level, including all 200 covered facilities required to meet federal energy mandates (42 U.S.C. 8253 - Energy management requirements (A+C?+I, or just A?)
    with an increasing percent of the total portfolio being monitored year by year Don't know where this is from??

    According to the EUAS data set, GSA has reduced the total number of buildings in their portfolio by 30% since 2003, and shifted a greater percent to Government Goal buildings (A category) each year.

  1. Count
    1. Total number of buildings in the EUAS data set by category

      2. Assumption: take the record of building category, "Cat", in the most recent year as the category designation for a building

      • A: government owned buildings subject to the energy reduction goals
      • B: government owned buildings exempt from the goals
      • I: energy intensive buildings (usually, but not always, owned) subject to the goals
      • C: Standard Active Lease
      • D: Exempt Leases
      • E: Reimbursable & Non-Reportable

      This reduction in total building numbers does not corresponds to a reduction of total square footage for the entire GSA portfolio, since on average buildings become larger in size.

      • The total square footage for the whole GSA portfolio increased by 1%.
      • The total square footage increased by 2% for A + C + I category.
      • The total square footage increased by 10% for the A + I category with at least 6 years of Electric EUI >= 12 kBtu/sq.ft and Gas EUI >= 3 kBtu/sq.ft from FY2007 to FY2015
    2. Covered facility by category and type

      3. A majority of the covered facilities are office buildings, followed by courthouses. There are 173 covered facilities within category A and I, and is subject to the energy reduction goal.

    4. Capital vs operational investment building count by year in EUAS data set (All buildings)

      5. To meet federal energy mandates of 30% EUI reductions from 2003 to 2015 for goal buildings (A + I), GSA has invested in energy metering as well as operational, capital and combined operational-capital upgrades to the A category building stock.

      6. Energy monitoring of GSA facilities has improved over time. However, there remain a significant number of buildings without gas meters, and some with inaccurate electric meters.
